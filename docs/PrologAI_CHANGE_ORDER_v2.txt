ENTRY ONE - WALL-3: causal_core could not yet mint the three new Causalontology 4.0.0 kinds

DISCOVERED: 2026-07-22, at the start of konnectome build slice 12 (the closure slice), recorded first as Wall-3 in konnectome_ledger_v1.txt.

THE FINDING. Causalontology 4.0.0 had just been released - its closed kind list grown from eighteen to twenty-one, now carrying three new kinds: attitude (holder, attitude_type, content), predicted_occurrence (instantiates, interval, predictor, strength), and prediction_error (predicted, observed, discrepancy); its conformance suite re-frozen at 137 vectors, with every 3.0.0 identifier preserved byte-for-byte. konnectome's closure slice needed to MINT records of those three kinds - the attributed belief as an attitude, the forecast as a predicted_occurrence, the signed surprise as a prediction_error - through its reused PrologAI library, and could not. PrologAI's causal_core 1.0.0 implemented only the eighteen 3.0.0 kinds: the predicate causal_core_identity_fields/2 carried no rows for attitude, predicted_occurrence, or prediction_error, so causal_core_identify failed outright on a record of any of the three, whether the kind was passed explicitly or inferred from the record's own type field.

WHY IT IS A LANGUAGE-LIBRARY GAP, NOT A DATA-STRUCTURE GAP. The distinction is the whole of the routing decision. This was NOT a Causalontology gap: the standard already carried all three kinds, correctly specified, at 4.0.0. It was NOT a konnectome-solvable gap either: konnectome may not edit its cousins in place, and reproducing the three kinds' identity rules inside a konnectome pack would fork the content-addressing away from the shared library, which is exactly the workaround the constitution forbids. The gap was the PrologAI LIBRARY lagging behind a shipped standard - causal_core implemented eighteen kinds while the standard now defined twenty-one. That is a real, load-bearing, additive PrologAI-language-library wall, and it is the first finding this change order has ever carried. The Ledger's third routing destination is used here for the first time.

CONSUMER-VERSUS-ONTOLOGY TEST: not applicable in the usual sense. That test asks whether a missing thing genuinely needs to be SHARED across minds and repositories (routing it to Causalontology) rather than merely computed in one runtime. Here the ontology already carried the kinds; the shareable record was already defined; only the library that mints it had not caught up. The gap was purely the language library's additive lag behind a released standard.

THE CHANGE (additive only). In packs/causal_core/prolog/causal_core.pl, three new causal_core_identity_fields/2 facts, one per new kind, mirroring the 4.0.0 schema: attitude -> [holder, attitude_type, content]; predicted_occurrence -> [instantiates, interval, predictor, strength]; prediction_error -> [predicted, observed, discrepancy]. The eighteen existing rows were left untouched, so every previously mintable record keeps its byte-for-byte identifier. The pack version moved 1.0.0 -> 1.1.0. The vendored conformance suite was re-vendored from Causalontology at the 4.0.0 commit (137 vectors), with new co_v/1 clauses mirroring the Python reference runner's semantics for the eighteen added vectors, and the harness counts moved from 119 to 137.

All Thirteenth-Commandment gates green before merge: the vendored Causalontology conformance suite exits at 137 of 137; the pack naming gate is clean; the swipl pack tests are green (the three new kinds round-trip, a strength-bearing predicted_occurrence gives a distinct identifier, a nested attitude whose content is another attitude's identifier identifies, and the Rule 24 dimension conflict raises the expected semantic error); and the honest mini regression is green - ARC-AGI-1 40 of 40 and ARC-AGI-2 12 of 12, stated exactly as a 10 percent spot-check with the full regression deferred, NEVER rounded up to a full-run claim, per REGRESSION_DEBT.md.
